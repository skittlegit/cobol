## Deliverable

### 1. Judge harness

Render the regulation clause plus every mutated locus with bounded context and
ask whether the example resembles drift that could occur in maintained legacy
COBOL rather than an artificial benchmark edit. Call a configurable
OpenAI-compatible endpoint and parse:

```json
{"verdict": "plausible|implausible|unsure", "reason": "..."}
```

Write one checkpointed row per successful verdict. Resume only rows matching
the current instance ID, drift type, stratum, model, model family, and prompt
contract. Source reconstruction must reproduce the checked-in catalogue and
manifest; a judge-only manifest stamp must not invalidate the generating-commit
provenance guard.

### 2. Rubric

`docs/tasks/T2.4-judging-rubric.md` defines plausible legacy drift, style
continuity, coherent dataflow, non-absurd values, and worked seed examples. A
mutation that compiles but targets representation syntax, contradicts its cited
rule, or leaves generator-looking dead scaffolding is not automatically
plausible.

### 3. Raw sample gate

Draw a deterministic seed-2400 sample of 50 current IDs, stratified across all
seven classes and both available strata. The gate is the **raw judge plausible
rate before human overrides**, and it passes iff that rate is at least 90%.
`unsure` is a miss for this gate. Record model, family, effort, seed, counts,
and the gate definition in both output and manifest.

If the sample fails, stop and report the rejection cluster. A new repair loop
requires technically substantive defects; do not rerun until randomness yields
a pass and do not tune superficial style to the judge.

### 4. Full run, adjudication, and drop policy

Judge the complete current catalogue, reusing only exact-compatible sample
rows. Apply these rules:

- raw `implausible` rows go to `rejected/implausible.jsonl`;
- every raw `unsure` row receives an explicit human verdict and rationale;
- accepted human overrides are logged in `judge_overrides.jsonl` and affect the
  shipping set only;
- overrides never change the raw gate; and
- report override count/rate as a headline datasheet field.

This is **Amendment A1**: the independent model is a plausibility screen, while
human adjudication owns the accepted ground truth without making the gate
self-certifying.

### 5. Human review

Review 15 current sample IDs against the rubric, spanning classes and strata,
and record judge/human agreement. The review file, full judgements, sample
judgements, adjudications, override log, accepted set, rejected sidecars, and
manifest must all describe the same catalogue.

## Gates

1. Raw 50-sample plausible rate ≥ 90% under a different model family.
2. Full output covers every current catalogue ID exactly once.
3. Human overrides are complete, rationalized, headline-reported, and excluded
   from the raw gate.
4. The 15-item review reports agreement.
5. Accepted + implausible + unresolved unsure equals the full catalogue, with no
   duplicated IDs.
6. Deterministic reconstruction, manifest provenance, schema, compiled
   validation, and Gate E regressions remain green.

## Completion evidence

The final 594-row compiled catalogue passed the current-ID Luna/high sample at
**50/50 (100%)** and the full raw gate at **557/594 (93.77%)**. Five `unsure`
rows were human-accepted under A1; the final drop policy is **562 accepted / 32
implausible / 0 unsure**, with **5/594 (0.84%)** overrides. Human review agrees
**15/15**. These values are recorded in the canonical artifacts and STATUS.
