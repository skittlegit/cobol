## Preconditions and shared decisions

T2.3 must be done and its gates green. Judge/verifier family must differ from
the Claude/Anthropic system-under-test family. T2.2/T2.3 locus ordering and
interprocedural decisions remain binding.

## Deliverable

1. **[CODE] Harness:** render clause text plus each mutated locus ±10 lines and
   ask whether it resembles drift that could occur in real legacy code or an
   artificial edit. Call a configurable OpenAI-compatible endpoint, parse a
   `{plausible|implausible|unsure, reason}` verdict, and write
   `data/benchmark/judgements.jsonl`. Refuse same-family judging in code.
2. **[CODE] Rubric:** define plausible legacy drift, style continuity, and
   non-absurd values, with three worked seed-corpus examples.
3. **[USER] Run:** with an OpenAI/Gemini key, run `benchmark-judge --sample 50`
   stratified across every class and both strata, then judge the full set.
4. **[CHAT] Spot-check:** review 15 of the 50 against the rubric and record
   agreement with the judge.
5. **Drop policy:** move implausible cases to a rejected sidecar with reasons;
   human-adjudicate `unsure` cases.

## Gate

At least 90% of the 50-item sample is plausible. The judge model and family are
documented in the benchmark manifest, and human agreement is reported.

50-sample, judge=<model>, agreement=Y/15)`.
