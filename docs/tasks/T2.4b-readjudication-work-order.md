## Stage 1.6 [CODE] — thin non-D4 shapes (executed, Lead-specified)
The seed-2400 judge rejected three D1/D3 shapes as artificial. Root causes were
**not** one defect: two distinct MO-1 bugs and one MO-3 bug. Per the Lead's
rulings, the operators' core transformations are untouched; what changed is the
**stale-value source** MO-1 draws from and its **site selection**, plus MO-3's
**shape repertoire** — all build-side policy, same pattern as the D4 `d4_mode`.

**(a) MO-1 stale values — off-grid synthesis.** `_stale_value` already preferred
a recorded prior version, but every clause's `prior_versions`/`prior_2022`
carried no numeric `value`, so it always fell to `current * 1.1` → the
unexplained 33-day threshold. Fixed by a per-`kind` grid of values the RBI
lineage actually uses, snapped to the **stale (laxer) side** — a rule that
tightened over time was looser before:

| kind | grid | example |
|---|---|---|
| `duration_days` | 7, 14, 15, 30, 45, 60, 90 | 30 → 45 |
| `duration_working_days` | 3, 7, 10 | 7 → 10 |
| `duration_years` | 1, 2, 5, 8, 10 | 2 → 5 |
| `duration_months` | 1, 2, 3, 6, 12 | — |
| `percentage` | 10, 15, 25 | 10 → 15 |
| `amount_inr` | 1-2-5 decade ladder | 500 → 1000 |

A recorded prior always wins over the grid. Which applied is stamped per
instance as `stale_source=prior_verified|grid_fallback` in
`provenance.mutation`, so the datasheet can report the **real-history vs.
plausible-synthesis ratio** rather than leave a reviewer to reconstruct it.

**Prior-value curation (Lead-supplied, T2.5 primary passes).** Of the four known
priors, exactly one is a numeric D1 target: **BO threshold 15% → 10%**
(2023-10-17), already wired in `KYC-bo-threshold.prior_versions` — it now
resolves as `prior_verified`. The other three are deliberately **not** MO-1
material and the non-numeric filter keeps them out: CKYCR update (prior is the
*absence* of a deadline — a D2 shape), closure-penalty day-basis (a basis
change, not a value), and the Ombudsman window ("one month" → 30 days — the
honest stale value is month arithmetic, i.e. the P3 boundary story). Per the

`mutation_ops`. Everything else has no real prior because the values carried

no truth exists to substitute.

## AMENDMENT A1 [PROPOSED — awaiting Track B Lead ratification] — judge vs human ground truth
The seed-2400 sample surfaced the disagreement T2.4 anticipated: Luna calls the
KYCSYNC3 neutered-SLA D2 shape "artificial"; Track B human adjudication ruled the
identical shape (`drift_056392`) **plausible**. Both cannot silently stand.

**Resolution — override-with-log, but the override does NOT count toward the gate.**
- **Gate** = the **raw** judge plausible rate (pre-override) ≥90% on the stratified
  50-sample. Unchanged in meaning: an independent automated screen.
- **Accepted set** = post-adjudication. Human adjudication may override the judge;
  every override is logged to `data/benchmark/judge_overrides.jsonl`
  (`instance_id`, `judge_verdict`, `human_verdict`, `rationale`), and the
  **override rate is reported as a headline datasheet number**.

**Rationale.** The judge is an automated plausibility *screen*, selected for being a
different model family — for independence, not authority. A stubbed validation
paragraph with dead upstream residue is the D2 premise itself; letting the screen
veto it would defer the benchmark's semantic ground truth to a model we chose
precisely because it is *not* authoritative. But counting overrides toward the gate
would make the gate self-certifying — we could override our way to green, which is
exactly the retro-fitting the M2 audit caught (BL-6). Splitting the two numbers keeps
human judgment in charge of what ships while leaving the gate an honest screen.

**Consequence (deliberate):** with overrides excluded, D4 repair alone yields
~44/50 = 88%. The thin non-D4 shapes are therefore load-bearing for the gate;
nothing is smuggled through.
