## ⚠ RE-ANCHOR to the newer regulation (2026-07-09, user-approved)

The T2.1 re-verification obligation ("re-verify CC amendment state on rbi.org.in") caught a
**silent poison** that a naive re-check would have missed. The consolidated 2022 MD page
(`id=12300`) still reads **"Last Updated: March 07, 2024"** — but that page is stale by design:
on **2025-11-28** RBI ran a wholesale restructuring and **repealed the 2022 Master Direction
for commercial banks**, reissuing it as the **RBI (Commercial Banks – Credit Cards and Debit
Cards: Issuance and Conduct) Directions, 2025** (Chapter VIII repeal clause, vide
`DOR.RRC.REC.302/33-01-010/2025-26`). Same 2025-11-28 wave produced the KYC Directions 2025.

CardDemo is a commercial-bank card-servicing system, so the 2025 Directions are the correct
in-force anchor. **Decision (user-approved): re-anchor the 16 CC records to the 2025
Directions.** What changed and what did not:

- **Values UNCHANGED** — 30-day activation, ₹500/**calendar**-day closure penalty, >3-day
  late-reporting, 30-day grievance, 1%/₹5000 cutoff, etc. all carried over verbatim (each
  confirmed against a reproduction of the 2025 text). So every `current_value` stands and was
  independently value-verified against the (now repealed) 2022 primary MD.
- **`doc` re-pointed** to `RBI-Commercial-Banks-CC-DC-Directions-2025`
  (matches `data/manifest.json` `clause_doc_id`; enforced by a gate test).
- **`clause_id` renumbered** to the 2025 paragraph scheme (paras 1–97, 8 chapters). The 2022
  origin is preserved per record in `check.prior_2022`. Mapping (2022 → 2025): 6(a)(vi)→11(6),
  6(a)(iv)→11(4), 6(b)(v)→12(5), 8(a)→19, 8(b)→20, 9(b)(ii)→23(2), 9(b)(v)→23(5),
  9(b)(vi)→23(6), 9(b)(vii)→23(7), 10(a)→24, 10(b)→25, 10(h)→31, 3(a)(xxi)→5(21), 26(c)→85,
  12(b)→36, **29→90 (the KYC/AML bridge)**.
- **`version`/`effective_date` = 2025-11-28** for all 16 (the in-force instrument's date). The
  value-change history (2022/2024) lives in `check.prior_2022` and the T6 registry, not in the
  current-state effective date.

**The earlier "clause 20 vs 29" correction is now historical:** it was correct *for the 2022
MD* (para 20 = co-branding outsourcing, para 29 = KYC/AML bridge). Under 2025, the bridge is
**paragraph 90**, and — a neat confirmation of why the confusion arose — 2025 **para 20 is the
unused-card-closure rule** (record `CC-08b`). The whole episode is why `record_id` is now a
**stable curation key decoupled from the (mutable) regulatory number**: reg numbers churn
across reissues; a stable internal key that survives renumbering is the right design.

**Provenance caveat:** the 2025 paragraph numbers are **secondary-mapped** (text reproduction,
fetched 2026-07-09); the RBI primary PDF is CAPTCHA-walled. Confirming the numbering against the
2025 primary PDF is a **T2.5 first-step obligation** (below). The *values* are primary-verified.

## CurrentValue conventions (the T2.1 vocabulary the schema comment delegates to Track B)

`CurrentValue` is frozen as `{kind, value}` with `extra="forbid"` — confirmed by gate failure
when qualifiers were placed top-level. Conventions:

- **kinds:** `duration_days`, `duration_working_days`, `duration_months`, `duration_years`,
  `amount_inr`, `percentage`, `multiplier`, `enum_set`, `boolean_required`, `formula`,
  `composite`.
- **Qualifiers** (`comparator`: `at_least` / `at_most` / `strictly_greater`; `note`) live
  **inside** `value` as `{"amount": X, "comparator": "..."}` — never as extra fields.
- **`composite`**: `value` is a dict of named sub-values, each itself `{kind, value}`-shaped.
  Consumers (T2.2 mutation targeting, T4.2 D1/D5 scoring) must handle both bare-scalar and
  qualified-dict `value` shapes.
- The `comparator` qualifier is what makes D5 objectively scorable: 9(b)(v)'s
  `strictly_greater` on 3 days is the canonical `>` vs `>=` ground truth.
